# Platform Hook Reference

Use this reference when the user asks to repurpose an article, create platform-specific hooks, or choose distribution angles.

Do not let social hook tone leak into the main article body.

## Canonical Hook Patterns

### Open-Loop Curiosity

Create an unfinished payoff so the next line feels necessary.

Good for:

- TikTok
- X
- Medium
- YouTube

Use carefully for serious articles. Best as a first-line hook, not as the article title.

### Contrarian Data Correction

Challenge weak consensus with evidence.

Good for:

- Rednote
- TikTok
- X
- Reddit
- LinkedIn
- Medium
- YouTube

Best when the user has proof, examples, or a precise operating insight.

### Situation-Tension-Reveal

Open with the situation, sharpen the tension, then reveal the lesson.

Good for:

- Rednote
- TikTok
- X
- Reddit
- LinkedIn
- Medium
- YouTube

Best for founder lessons, case studies, and operational breakdowns.

### Proof-Led Claim

Lead with visible evidence, result, or specific observation before interpretation.

Good for:

- Rednote
- TikTok
- Instagram
- Reddit
- YouTube

Best for reports, benchmarks, tests, experiments, and screenshots.

### Authority Through Specificity

Use exact numbers, decisions, constraints, or tradeoffs.

Good for:

- Rednote
- X
- Instagram
- LinkedIn
- Medium
- YouTube

Best for expert articles and operator-style writing.

### Confession To Lesson

Turn a mistake, awkward truth, or changed belief into a useful takeaway.

Good for:

- TikTok
- X
- Instagram
- Reddit
- LinkedIn

Best for founder voice and personal lessons.

### Timely Shift

Lead with a recent change and explain what it changes for the reader.

Good for:

- X
- LinkedIn
- Medium
- YouTube

Best for AI/search platform changes, market shifts, and new operating constraints.

### Reader Categorization

Sort readers into recognizable groups so they self-identify.

Good for:

- Rednote
- X
- Instagram
- LinkedIn
- Medium

Best for decision guides and "which approach fits you" content.

## Platform Guidance

### X

Use:

- sharp first line
- contrarian or timely hook
- thread-friendly structure
- specific claims and compact examples
- the article's core claim in the first 1-2 lines
- one concrete example, scenario, or diagnostic by the third post in a thread
- short lines that can be scanned without opening the article

Avoid:

- long setup
- definition-first openings unless the definition is surprising
- generic thought leadership language

### LinkedIn

Use:

- professional story arc
- clear business implication
- authority through specificity
- paragraph rhythm that is easy to scan
- the article's core claim connected to an operational consequence
- one example, before/after, or decision rule before the midpoint

Avoid:

- fake vulnerability
- inflated leadership language
- generic "AI is changing everything" framing

### Reddit

Use:

- direct, plainspoken premise
- practical context
- specific tradeoffs
- question or discussion framing when useful

Avoid:

- sales tone
- polished marketing language

### Medium

Use:

- article-first title
- clear narrative progression
- frameworks and examples
- deeper explanation than social channels

Avoid:

- thread-like formatting

### YouTube

Use:

- title with concrete promise
- script outline or chapter beats
- open loop with practical payoff

Avoid:

- vague "why this matters" framing without a visible payoff

### TikTok / Instagram / Rednote

Use:

- visual-first hook
- specific before/after or mistake/lesson framing
- short script beats
- concrete production notes

Avoid:

- dense abstractions
- article-style intros

## Distribution Output Shape

When requested, add:

```markdown
Distribution hooks
- X: ...
- LinkedIn: ...
- Reddit: ...

Repurpose notes
- ...
```

Keep hooks platform-specific. Do not produce the same sentence with platform names swapped.
