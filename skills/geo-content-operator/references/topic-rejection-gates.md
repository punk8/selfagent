# Topic Rejection Gates

Use this before handing a topic to `geo-article-writer`.

## Hard Reject

Reject or revise if:

- the topic is only a broad definition and the keyword has already been covered
- the core claim is obvious, for example "AI search optimization is important"
- the article cannot name a specific reader decision, workflow, or failure mode
- the proposed examples require real customer proof that is not available
- the same keyword, lane, and artifact were used in a recent draft
- requested visuals would be decorative only
- the article depends on live data but no research tool or user-supplied evidence is available

## Revise Instead Of Reject

Revise when:

- the keyword is useful but the angle is generic
- the reader is too broad
- the core claim lacks tension
- the article has examples but no diagnostic or decision rule
- the article has a framework but no concrete application

## Approval Criteria

Approve when the brief has:

- one visible, debatable core claim
- a clear content lane
- a reason this is not a duplicate
- a proof posture that does not fake evidence
- a concrete artifact: framework, diagnostic, comparison, checklist, before/after example, or source-backed finding
- a visual plan when images are requested

## Better Claim Patterns

Use patterns like:

- "The first GEO gap is not content volume; it is missing recommendation context."
- "AI search optimization starts with a recommendation contract, not a keyword map."
- "B2B SaaS pages fail AI answers when they describe features but not fit conditions."
- "The page that wins AI search is the page an assistant can safely cite for a buyer decision."
