# Content Mix

Use this to prevent the blog from becoming a stream of similar GEO explainers.

## Primary Lanes

### Category Authority

Use when the article should define or own a market concept.

Good for:

- GEO, AEO, AI search optimization, AI visibility
- explaining buyer behavior changes
- creating citation-worthy definitions

Risk:

- generic "what is" articles with no operational insight

### Competitor Or Alternative

Use when the article compares methods, tools, workflows, or buying criteria.

Good for:

- "monitoring vs optimization"
- "keyword SEO vs AI search optimization"
- "dashboard vs workflow"

Risk:

- shallow comparison without evaluation criteria

### Adjacency Or Ecosystem

Use when the article enters the buyer's surrounding workflow.

Good for:

- content operations
- product marketing
- sales enablement
- help docs and use-case pages

Risk:

- losing the GEO connection

### Product-Led Proof

Use when the article can show how the product helps.

Good for:

- audit workflows
- citation gap diagnosis
- page-level recommendations
- reporting and prioritization

Risk:

- turning the article into a product page

## Rotation Guidance

Across a rolling set of 10 substantial articles, aim for:

- 5 reinforcement articles around core category keywords
- 4 promotion or product-adjacent articles
- 1 acquisition article for a new audience or adjacent problem

Do not publish three product-led proof articles in a row unless the user explicitly wants a campaign sequence.

## Mention Intensity

Use one:

- direct: the article names the product or category solution as part of the answer
- comparative: the article compares product-relevant approaches without hard selling
- ambient: the product logic appears through examples and diagnostics
- none: the article is pure education or category authority

Default to ambient or comparative for top-of-funnel articles. Use direct only when the goal asks for product-led proof.
